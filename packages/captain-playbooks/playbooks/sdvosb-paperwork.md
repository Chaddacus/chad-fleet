# SDVOSB Business Setup and Veteran Benefits

## Summary

Covers the paperwork, registrations, elections, and benefits available to a service-disabled
veteran-owned small business in Georgia with a full VA disability rating. Concrete steps with
deadlines and dollar amounts. Specific to Chad's situation: SDVOSB-eligible Air Force veteran
with full disability rating, Georgia resident, LLC or S-corp considering S-corp election.

Fires when a new business entity is being formed, a commercialization milestone is approaching,
or a benefit has not been activated.

## When to consult

- A new LLC or S-corp is being formed and optimal entity structure has not been decided.
- SAM.gov registration is not complete or is approaching its annual renewal deadline.
- SDVOSB certification has not been completed or VA-verified status has not been confirmed.
- S-corp election timing is being evaluated as net profit approaches the $60-80K range.
- CHAMPVA coverage has not been verified for eligible family members.
- The GA Business Certificate of Exemption has not been applied for.
- VR&E Self-Employment Track enrollment is being evaluated.

## Recommendations

### VA disability and SDVOSB eligibility

1. **Verify your VA disability rating letter is on file and current.**

   The SDVOSB certification process at VA CVE requires the disability rating letter issued by
   the VA Regional Office. The letter must state the service-connected disability percentage.
   Any percentage qualifies for SDVOSB; 10%+ qualifies as "service-disabled."

   The letter must be the most recent rating decision, not a benefits summary letter. Download
   from VA.gov > My Health > VA Letters. Store with business formation documents.

   Measurable outcome: current disability rating letter downloaded, stored in the secure
   documents folder with the date of the letter recorded.

2. **Complete VA-verified SDVOSB certification through SAM.gov.**

   Self-certification is not sufficient for VA set-asides. The VA CVE process requires:
   (a) DD-214 showing honorable discharge,
   (b) VA disability rating letter,
   (c) business formation documents showing your name as owner,
   (d) proof that the veteran controls day-to-day operations and owns at least 51%.

   As of 2023, this is done through the certification module in SAM.gov. Processing time:
   45-90 days. Do not wait until a contract opportunity is in hand.

   Measurable outcome: SAM.gov profile shows SDVOSB as "Verified" (not "Self-Certified")
   with the expiration date tracked in the calendar.

### Business entity structure

3. **Operate as a Georgia LLC until net profit consistently exceeds $60-80K per year.**

   A single-member LLC in Georgia is a disregarded entity for federal taxes. All net profit
   flows to Schedule C and is subject to self-employment tax at 15.3% on the first ~$168K.

   When net profit exceeds $60-80K annually, an S-corp election lets you split income between
   a reasonable salary and a distribution, avoiding SE tax on the distribution. At $80K net
   profit, the S-corp election saves approximately $4,000-8,000/year — more than the accounting
   overhead.

   File IRS Form 2553 within 75 days of the start of the tax year in which S-corp treatment
   should take effect.

   Measurable outcome: quarterly review of trailing 12-month net profit; initiate S-corp
   process when it exceeds $65K.

4. **Confirm the Georgia LLC articles of organization are complete and accurate.**

   Filing fee: $100 online. The registered agent must have a physical Georgia address. The LLC
   operating agreement must explicitly state that the SDVOSB-eligible veteran owns 51%+ and
   controls day-to-day management decisions. This language is required for VA CVE verification.

   Check good-standing status on the Georgia Secretary of State's online registry annually.

   Measurable outcome: Georgia LLC shows active/good-standing status on the SOS registry
   with correct ownership language in the operating agreement.

### Georgia-specific benefits

5. **Apply for the Georgia Business Certificate of Exemption for veteran-owned businesses.**

   Georgia offers a 10-year local business license fee waiver for service-disabled veteran-owned
   businesses under OCGA 43-47-3(d). Application goes to your county's business license office.

   Bring: VA disability rating letter, business registration documents, and Georgia photo ID.
   The fee waiver applies to local occupational tax certificates — county and city — not
   state-level registrations. Annual renewal is typically a one-page form.

   Measurable outcome: certificate obtained and stored with business formation documents;
   annual renewal date in calendar.

6. **Separate VA compensation income from business income in your accounting system.**

   Georgia does not have a special veteran income tax exclusion for business income. However,
   VA disability compensation is fully excluded from Georgia state income tax (and federal).

   Mixing VA compensation and business revenue in the same QuickBooks or Wave income category
   creates tax documentation risk. They must be tracked in separate categories from day one.

   Measurable outcome: accounting system has a dedicated "VA Disability Compensation" income
   category separate from all business revenue categories.

### VR&E Self-Employment Track

7. **Evaluate VR&E Chapter 31 Self-Employment Track if not already enrolled.**

   The VA's Vocational Rehabilitation and Employment program Chapter 31 includes a
   Self-Employment Track for veterans with service-connected disabilities pursuing
   self-employment. Benefits can include: business plan support, initial operating cost
   funding, tools and equipment, training, and ongoing case management.

   File VA Form 28-1900 at your regional VA office or online at VA.gov. The process takes
   4-8 weeks to get a counselor assigned.

   Measurable outcome: VR&E eligibility determination completed and enrollment decision made,
   even if use is deferred until a specific equipment or training need arises.

### SAM.gov registration and maintenance

8. **Renew SAM.gov registration annually, 60 days before expiration.**

   SAM.gov registrations expire exactly 1 year from activation. No grace period exists.
   An expired registration disqualifies you from award and can prevent payment on existing
   contracts.

   Renewal takes 1-2 weeks if entity information is unchanged, up to 4 weeks if there are
   address or ownership changes. Set two reminders: 60 days and 30 days before expiration.

   Measurable outcome: SAM.gov expiration date in the calendar with a 60-day and 30-day
   reminder event.

### CHAMPVA family healthcare

9. **Activate CHAMPVA for eligible family members.**

   CHAMPVA provides healthcare coverage to spouses and dependent children of veterans with a
   100% permanent and total (P&T) VA disability rating. Coverage pays 75% of costs after a
   $50/year deductible per beneficiary ($100/year family cap). No monthly premium.

   To enroll: submit VA Form 10-10d with:
   - The veteran's VA rating letter showing P&T status
   - Dependent's birth certificate or marriage certificate
   - Social Security numbers for all enrollees

   Processing time: 6-8 weeks. CHAMPVA is secondary if dependents have other coverage.

   Measurable outcome: CHAMPVA application submitted for all eligible dependents; welcome
   packet received and coverage effective date recorded.

### S-corp election and tax planning

10. **Set the reasonable salary at 40-50% of net profit when electing S-corp.**

    The IRS requires S-corp owner-employees to pay themselves a "reasonable salary" before
    taking distributions. A common safe harbor: salary = 40-50% of net profit. This avoids the
    red flag of a $1 salary with $100K in distributions.

    Work with a CPA experienced with S-corp owner-operators. The first-year election and salary
    determination must be done with professional guidance.

    Estimated accounting cost: $1,500-3,000/year for bookkeeping and S-corp tax return
    (Form 1120-S).

    Measurable outcome: reasonable salary documented in corporate minutes and payroll system
    established before the first S-corp distribution.

11. **Establish a Solo 401(k) or SEP-IRA in the year of S-corp election.**

    As an S-corp owner-employee, you can contribute to a Solo 401(k) as both employer (up to
    25% of W-2 salary) and employee (up to $23,000 in 2024 plus $7,500 catch-up if over 50).
    A SEP-IRA is simpler: 25% of compensation up to $69,000 in 2024.

    These contributions reduce federal taxable income dollar-for-dollar. At a 30% effective
    rate, a $20,000 contribution saves approximately $6,000 in taxes.

    Measurable outcome: retirement account opened in the same tax year as the S-corp election.

## Anti-patterns

- Waiting until you win a federal contract to complete SAM.gov registration. Processing delays
  will prevent award and delay payment.
- Using self-certification for SDVOSB on VA set-aside contracts. The VA's Veterans First
  Contracting Program rejects self-certified businesses.
- Electing S-corp before net profit exceeds $60K. The accounting overhead ($1,500-3,000/year)
  is not justified until SE tax savings exceed that cost.
- Setting a $1/year salary as an S-corp owner. This is a known IRS audit trigger.
- Mixing VA disability compensation and business income in the same accounting category.
  Creates tax documentation risk and mischaracterizes non-taxable income.
- Skipping CHAMPVA for eligible dependents. This is a significant benefit with no ongoing
  premium. The only cost is filing the paperwork.
- Missing the VR&E enrollment window. The support is most useful during initial business
  infrastructure buildout; retroactive requests are harder to justify.

## Decision rubric

| Situation | Recommended action |
|---|---|
| Net profit under $60K/year | Stay as single-member LLC; defer S-corp election |
| Net profit $60-80K/year | Model the S-corp tax savings with a CPA; elect if savings exceed $3K |
| Net profit above $80K/year | S-corp election almost certainly worth it; act within the 75-day window |
| VA rating is 100% P&T | Apply for CHAMPVA for all eligible dependents immediately |
| SAM.gov registration near expiration | Renew now; do not wait for an active opportunity |

# Indie Author Launch Playbook

## Summary

Covers the full lifecycle of a self-published book launch on Amazon KDP: cover and metadata
preparation, ARC program execution, launch week tactics, and post-launch ranking velocity.
Fires when a new title is within 90 days of release or an existing title needs a relaunch push.

## When to consult

- A new book is set to release within 90 days and no pre-launch checklist has been started.
- ARC copies have not gone out at least 3 weeks before launch date.
- The book is live but has fewer than 10 reviews in the first 30 days.
- Amazon rank has fallen out of the top 5,000 in its primary category and no ad campaign is active.
- A price promotion or Kindle Countdown Deal is being planned without a coordinated email blast.
- A title has been live for 90+ days with under 25 reviews and no active outreach.

## Recommendations

### Pre-launch (90 to 15 days out)

1. **Lock the cover before anything else.**

   Commission cover design at least 60 days out. The cover must signal genre in 0.5 seconds at
   thumbnail size — test it at 80x80 pixels. A cover that looks like literary fiction when the
   book is cozy mystery will kill launch week CTR regardless of ad spend.

   Test with at least 3 genre-familiar readers who do not know your premise. Ask only: "What
   genre is this?" If more than one person guesses wrong, the cover fails.

   Measurable outcome: 3 genre-informed readers confirm genre in a cold cover test before you
   finalize.

2. **Write the blurb with the AIDA structure.**

   Attention (hook to the conflict), Interest (deepen stakes), Desire (promise of payoff),
   Action (call to download or buy). Blurbs that front-load character description before
   conflict underperform consistently. Use Dave Chesson's Kindlepreneur blurb builder as a
   reference frame.

   Test at least two versions on your email list or in a Facebook reader group poll. Pick the
   version with the higher "would read" signal.

   Measurable outcome: final blurb selected based on a test, with >60% positive response from
   genre-familiar readers.

3. **Build your ARC list to 30-50 readers minimum.**

   Use BookFunnel ARC program or StoryOrigin Group Promotions. Target readers who have left
   reviews on comparable titles — same genre, similar page count. Offer ARC copies 4-6 weeks
   before launch so reviewers have time to finish and post.

   Send one follow-up reminder at the 10-day mark. Aim for a 40-60% conversion rate from ARC
   download to posted review.

   Measurable outcome: 15-25 reviews live on launch day.

4. **Set up Amazon Author Central completely.**

   Author photo, bio (150-200 words, written in third person), editorial reviews section
   populated with at least one blurb from a credible source. Link your blog or newsletter.

   A complete Author Central page increases click-through from author search results. An
   incomplete page signals to browsers that the author is not seriously invested in their
   product.

   Measurable outcome: all fields populated before the book goes live for pre-order.

5. **Load the pre-order at $3.99 or higher, 30-45 days before launch.**

   Pre-orders accumulate toward the single launch-day sales rank spike. Set the launch day
   price on the pre-order. Do not set a pre-order price at $0.99 — it signals low value and
   trains buyers to anchor to that price.

   Use the pre-order period to gather newsletter signups by promoting the upcoming release.
   Even a 25-person list is a meaningful signal on launch day.

   Measurable outcome: at least 25 pre-orders secured before launch day.

6. **Build the keyword and category strategy using Publisher Rocket.**

   Select 7 KDP keywords that match actual search terms readers use — not genre labels like
   "romance" or "thriller." Target 3-7 word phrases. Choose 2 categories, at least one of
   which has a Best Seller threshold below 2,000 rank — meaning a book at #2,000 or better
   holds #1 in that category.

   Measurable outcome: both categories identified and loaded into the KDP dashboard before
   launch. All 7 keyword slots filled with search-validated phrases.

### Launch week (day -1 through day +7)

7. **Price at $0.99 for launch day only, then raise to $3.99 or full price on day 2.**

   The $0.99 price on day one drives volume to spike rank. The 20Books community and
   Kindlepreneur data both confirm the rank spike is more valuable than per-unit revenue on
   day one.

   Raise to full price immediately after the 24-hour window. Do not run $0.99 for more than
   36 hours or you train readers to wait for sales and reduce the signal of your full-price
   demand.

   Measurable outcome: rank spike visible in primary category within 24 hours of launch.

8. **Send a launch-day newsletter at 9 AM Eastern.**

   Subject line: the book title and a single sentence that names the conflict. Include a
   direct Amazon buy link. Do not include more than one CTA. Do not include images that slow
   load time.

   Send a second email on day 3 if open rate was above target, announcing review milestones.
   Open rate target: >35% for a warm list. Click rate target: >8%.

   Measurable outcome: both emails sent within the first 72 hours, open and click data logged.

9. **Post to social in sequence: newsletter > Facebook reader group > personal page > Twitter/Bluesky.**

   Stagger by 30-60 minutes. Reply to every comment on launch day — comment velocity signals
   algorithmic distribution on most platforms. Do not announce on every channel simultaneously;
   staggered posts generate more total engagement than a single simultaneous burst.

   Measurable outcome: at least 50 social interactions (likes + comments) in the first 24 hours.

10. **Post in relevant Facebook reader groups only where explicitly permitted.**

    Read pinned rules before posting. Use paid or free promotion threads only. Never post in a
    "no promo" group. Target groups with 10K+ members focused on your exact genre.

    Measurable outcome: 3-5 relevant group posts in launch week with zero rules violations.

### Post-launch (days 8-90)

11. **Start Amazon Sponsored Products ads on day 8 at a $5/day budget.**

    Keyword targeting: use Publisher Rocket's AMS keyword tool to pull 50-100 high-relevance,
    low-bid keywords. ACOS target: <70% in weeks 1-2 while building history, then optimize
    toward <50% by week 6.

    Do not run product targeting (ASIN targeting) until you have at least 10 reviews. Without
    social proof, CTR will be too low to generate actionable data.

    Measurable outcome: at least 200 impressions per day by day 14.

12. **Push the review count to 25+ by day 30.**

    Email your list again at day 14 with a brief "reviews help more than you know" message.
    Include direct links to leave a review on Amazon and Goodreads. Follow up with ARC readers
    who have not posted.

    Measurable outcome: 25 reviews by day 30.

13. **Optimize for also-boughts.**

    Read books in your genre on the same Kindle account used for your author profile. This
    feeds Amazon's algorithm with accurate co-read signals. Avoid buying books in unrelated
    genres on your author account.

    Measurable outcome: also-bought shelf shows at least 5 titles in the same genre
    sub-category within 45 days.

14. **Run a Kindle Countdown Deal at day 45-60.**

    Price to $0.99 for 3 days, then back to full. Promote via BookSirens, Bargain Booksy, or
    Robin Reads for the deal window. Books require 25+ reviews for BookBub and most major
    lists; Bargain Booksy accepts at 5+.

    Measurable outcome: minimum 50 paid downloads during the Countdown window.

## Anti-patterns

- Launching before the ARC program delivers reviews. Reviews on page on launch day are the
  primary trust signal. Zero reviews on launch day kills the rank spike.
- Using all 7 KDP keyword slots for single-word genre terms. These are too competitive and
  generate zero meaningful traffic. Use 3-7 word search phrases.
- Setting $0.99 permanently as the book price. It trains the reader base to wait for sales,
  reduces KU page-read rates relative to unit sales, and signals low production value.
- Running ads before you have 10+ reviews. ACOS will be destructive and you will quit before
  the campaign has enough data to optimize.
- Sending newsletters without a direct link to the buy page. Every friction point between
  "read email" and "click buy" destroys conversion.
- Buying reviews or enrolling in review swaps. This violates Amazon TOS and triggers account
  flags that can result in listing removal.
- Chasing a BookBub Featured Deal before the title has 25 reviews and a 4.0+ rating.
  Applications before those thresholds are wasted effort.
- Changing the cover or blurb in launch week. Wait at least 90 days post-launch to test
  metadata changes.

## Decision rubric

| Situation | Recommended action |
|---|---|
| Launch day, under 5 reviews live | Delay public ads 7 days; push ARC follow-up immediately |
| Rank below 10,000 after day 7 | Activate Countdown Deal window immediately |
| Ad ACOS above 100% after 2 weeks | Pause product targeting; switch to keyword-only targeting |
| Open rate below 20% on launch email | Resend to non-openers with a different subject line |
| Category rank #1 achieved | Screenshot and add to Amazon Editorial Reviews section |
| Under 15 ARC reviews at launch | Focus on review push before ad spend; ads with no reviews waste budget |
